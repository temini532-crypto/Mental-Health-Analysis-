# Student Mental Health Analysis Dashboard

## Project Overview

Mental health has become an increasingly important concern among university students, affecting academic performance, social relationships, and overall well-being. This project explores factors associated with students' mental health by analyzing survey responses collected from university students.

Using Power BI, the dataset was cleaned, transformed, and visualized to uncover patterns relating to depression, anxiety, panic attacks, treatment seeking behavior, gender, age, academic performance (CGPA), marital status, and year of study.

The goal of this project is to demonstrate how data analytics can support evidence based decision making and provide insights that may assist educational institutions in identifying vulnerable student populations.

---

## Objectives

This project aims to:

* Analyze the prevalence of depression, anxiety, and panic attacks among students.
* Examine the relationship between mental health and demographic characteristics.
* Explore the influence of academic performance on mental health.
* Identify the proportion of students who sought professional treatment.
* Build an interactive dashboard that allows users to explore mental health trends through filters and visualizations.

---

## Dataset

## Data Cleaning and Preparation

The following transformations were carried out in Power Query and DAX:

* Removed duplicate records.
* Checked and handled missing values.
* Standardized categorical values.
* Converted Timestamp into a Date field.
* Created Year and Month columns for analysis.
* Verified data types across all fields.
* Created calculated measures for dashboard KPIs.

## Key Insights

The analysis revealed several important findings:

* Mental health conditions affect a significant proportion of surveyed students.
* Anxiety was one of the most commonly reported mental health concerns.
* Mental health experiences varied across gender and academic disciplines.
* Only a portion of affected students sought professional treatment.
* Academic performance alone does not fully explain mental health outcomes, suggesting that multiple social and personal factors contribute to student well-being.
